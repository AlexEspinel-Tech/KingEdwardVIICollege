    • ICT50220

    Diploma of Information Technology (Cyber Security) – King Edward VII College

    Field Details
    Duration 65 weeks (for international students)
    Delivery mode On-campus classroom & self-study
    Units included Total units: 20 (6 core units + 14 elective units)
    Core units:
    BSBCRT512 Originate and develop concepts
    BSBXCS402 Promote workplace cyber security awareness and best practices
    BSBXTW401 Lead and facilitate a team
    ICTICT517 Match ICT needs with the strategic direction of the organisation
    ICTICT532 Apply IP, ethics and privacy policies in ICT environments
    ICTSAS527 Manage client problems

    Elective units (selected examples):
    ICTCYS407 Gather, analyse and interpret threat data
    ICTCYS610 Protect critical infrastructure for organisations
    ICTCYS613 Utilise design methodologies for security architecture
    ICTSAS524 Develop, implement and evaluate an incident response plan
    ICTSAS526 Review and update disaster recovery and contingency plans
    ICTNWK529 Install and manage complex ICT networks
    ICTNWK546 Manage network security
    Entry requirements • Minimum age: 18 years (for international students)
    • Completion of at least Year 12 or equivalent qualification
    • IELTS Academic score of 6.0 overall (or equivalent) — test results must be no more than 2 years old
    • Participation in a course entry interview to demonstrate suitability
    • English language competence may be exempted if any of the following apply:
    – 5 years of education in an English-speaking country – Completed at least 6 months of a Certificate IV level course at an Australian RTO – Completed an English placement test and digital literacy test – Completed high school in English language
    Fees The tuition fee for international students is approximately AUD $13,600 per year (for this course).
    (Exact fees should be confirmed via the “View Course Fees” link on the website.)
